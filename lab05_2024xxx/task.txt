Implement an 8-bit floating point addition circuit which takes as input
two 8-bit floating point numbers and outputs their sum which is also 
an 8-bit floating point number. 

For this exercise you don't need to handle special cases, i.e., overflows,
underflows, zero, non-normalized numbers, +/- inifinities, NaNs, etc. 
Don't care about the round-up as well; discard the extra bits to the right
if any. Just assume that you'll get two normalized floating point numbers
and their normalized sum will also be in range.
 
Assume that your 8-bit floating point representation uses:
- The msb b[7] for sign bit
- Next 4 bits b[6:3] for exponent stored in biased format
  (guess range, precision, and the value of the bias yourself)
  The exponents 0x0 and 0xF are reserved.
- The last 3 lsbs b[0:2] for mantissa.

Remember, there's an implicit 1 to the left of binary point.


You may take help from internet but you should be able to explain every line
of code you submit. 

Make some test cases to check your implementation.
-  2.75 + -4 (0 1000 011 + 1 1001 000)
-  5.5 + 44 (0 1001 011 + 0 1100 011)
-  2.25 + 2.5 (0 1000 001 + 0 1000 010)

Make a couple of other test cases as well.
